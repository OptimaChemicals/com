STIMULATION CHECMICALS

ENHANCE STIMULATION WITH STANDARD AND CUSTOMIZED ADDITIVES BACKED BY FIELD-PROVEN CHEMISTRY

Optima Chemicals provides a broad selection of cost-effective and readily accessible well-stimulation additives for hydraulic fracturing and acidizing, all designed to enhance performance and ensure protection.

With decades of experience in development and manufacturing, we produce additives that deliver superior performance. We guarantee not only the compatibility of our additives but also their efficacy, ensuring optimal performance and stimulation of your well.

 

UNLOCK YOUR WELL'S POTENTIAL WITH OPTIMA CHEMICAL'S LINE OF TOP-PERFORMING WELL STIMULATION PRODUCTS.

Optima Chemicals manufactures and supplies a comprehensive range of stimulation chemicals for matrix and fracture acidizing treatments in sandstone and carbonate reservoirs. These treatments address and often completely resolve formation damage caused by common well operations such as perforating, cementing, and drilling. Leveraging our extensive expertise in acid chemicals, formation damage, and acid stimulation mechanics, we design effective solutions to mitigate or eliminate these issues, thereby enhancing well productivity.

Optima Chemicals's acidizing additives are effective over a wide temperature range and for a variety of acid blends:

i. Acid corrosion inhibitors protect metal surfaces and minimize damage to assets.

ii. Acid corrosion inhibitor intensifiers increase the effectiveness of inhibitors at higher temperatures.

iii. Acid retarders/emulsifiers slow down acid reaction time to ensure maximum penetration of live acid into the formation.

iv. Anti-sludge agents prevent acid sludge formation to improve cleanup.

v. Friction reducers reduce induced fluid turbulence in tubulars, which decreases horsepower requirements.

vi. Acid-gelling agents reduce fluid loss in the formation and increase fracture conductivity.

vii. Mutual solvents prevent acid and crude oil emulsions, enhance water-wetting properties, and improve cleanup.

viii. Iron control / sequestering agents prevent precipitation of iron from spent acid fluids.

ix. Surfactants reduce surface and interfacial tension, help prevent emulsions, alter formation wettability, and improve flowback.

x. Foaming agents created for the intention of stimulating liquid-sensitive formations. form voluminous, highly stable foams within fracturing fluids.

xi. Formation stabilizers intended to prevent formation damage that can result from the swelling and/or migration of clay.

xii. Water gelling agents intended for the purposes of decreasing friction pressure, and achieving increased viscosity through crosslinking.

xiii. Crosslinkers work effectively to boost viscosity, minimize water requirements, and improve proppant placement.

xiv. Breakers intended for the purpose of breaking crosslinks to lower a fluidâ€™s viscosity, degrading water gels back to a watery consistency.

xv. Hydrogen sulfide scavengers engineered to boost the effectiveness of acid corrosion inhibiters in the presence of hydrogen sulfide (H2S).

xvi. Biocides quickly and effectively deliver microbiological control in a range of applications.

xvii. Scale inhibitors developed to prevent calcium sulfate, barium sulfate and calcium carbonate scale formation.
